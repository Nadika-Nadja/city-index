Most Indian city rankings serve political or commercial interests. Swachh Bharat, Smart Cities, EoDB indices — all carry incentive structures that reward compliance theatre over ground truth. National vs state political dynamics further distort outcomes. This ranking tries to measure what a median resident actually experiences, not what a press release claims. Scores reflect lived reality, river health, median wage ratios, and patterns visible to people who aren't visiting the showcase zones.

Dimensions

Leisure: entertainment breadth, access across income levels
Openness: women's safety at night, minority experience, caste texture, cross-community mixing
Heritage: depth, layering, living vs museum quality

On the Marketing vs Reality Gap

Measures divergence between official/media narrative and ground experience. High gap = city is systematically oversold relative to what a median resident experiences. Includes: Swachh Bharat rankings vs river/drain health; Smart City claims vs infrastructure; EoDB scores vs actual business experience; tourism branding vs safety reality.

Methodology Note

Scores are opinionated assessments cross-referenced against: WHO air quality data, CPCB river monitoring, resident surveys, journalistic ground reports, international comparison benchmarks. No government ranking was used as a primary source.
