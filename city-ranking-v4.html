8 Scored Dimensions

Transport: network coverage, cost as % of min wage, first/last mile, all-class access
Public Space: free access, walking distance, shade, class diversity of actual users
Cleanliness: visible hygiene, drain function, garbage collection in non-showcase areas
Cost vs Life: median rent/wage ratio, public healthcare, food, schooling access
Economy Scale: size, depth, global connectivity
Economy Resilience: sector diversity, shock absorption, informal economy health
Leisure: entertainment breadth across income levels
Openness: women's safety at night, minority experience, caste texture, social mixing
Heritage: depth, civilisational continuity, living vs museum quality

🌿 Ecology — Standalone Indicator

Not a score feeder. A separate civilisational stress signal.

Sub-indicators: River/water body health (CPCB monitoring, not declared quality) · Groundwater trajectory (CGWB data) · Green cover 10-year change (satellite-derived) · Air quality annual PM2.5 burden · Government initiative quality (measured by whether indicators are reversing, not by schemes announced)

A city can score well on Cleanliness (streets swept) while scoring poorly on Ecology (river dead, groundwater critical). These are different phenomena.

On Heritage Scoring

Corrected for systematic bias in Indian rankings that overweights Mughal-era monument aesthetics. This index weights: civilisational continuity, living heritage (tradition still practiced), and geographic footprint (did this culture influence regions beyond itself). Dravidian temple urbanism, maritime Christian heritage, and syncretic court traditions score accordingly.

On the Marketing Gap

High gap = city systematically oversold vs median resident experience. Measures divergence between official/tourism narrative and ground reality across all dimensions.
