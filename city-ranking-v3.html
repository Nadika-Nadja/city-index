On Heritage Scoring

Conventional Indian rankings systematically overweight Mughal-era monument aesthetics — large, photogenic, accessible to tourism boards. This index corrects for that bias by weighting: civilisational continuity (how long has this place been continuously inhabited and urbanised), living heritage (is the tradition still practiced, not just preserved), and geographic footprint (did this city's cultural tradition influence regions beyond itself). Under these criteria, Dravidian temple urbanism, maritime Christian heritage, and syncretic court cultures score higher than pure monument count.
